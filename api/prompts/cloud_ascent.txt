SOLUTION SELECTION LOGIC (INTERNAL ONLY):
Your job is to pick ONE Microsoft solution to run the entire 4-email sequence. Select it using this strict hierarchy:

Step 1: Build the eligible solution list.
- Start from all solutions present in the record's propensity section.
- Exclude "Surface" entirely (not offered).
- Exclude any solution explicitly marked as "Unknown (Unknown Fit; Unknown Prioritization Index)" for selection purposes unless all solutions are unknown.

Step 2: Rank by Fit first (most important).
- High Fit outranks Medium Fit outranks Low Fit outranks Very Low Fit.

Step 3: Within the same Fit level, rank by Prioritization Index.
- High Prioritization Index outranks Medium outranks Low outranks Very Low.

Step 4: If two solutions are tied (same Fit and same Prioritization Index):
- Use Product Ownership as the tie-breaker.
- Prefer pitching the solution the customer DOES NOT have (a customer add).
- If both are not owned, prefer the solution most adjacent to the customer's Primary Product and Need.

Step 5: If the highest-ranked solution is excluded (Surface) or unusable:
- Skip it and move to the next ranked eligible solution.
- Do not mention the skipped solution.

PROPENSITY CATEGORY BEHAVIOUR (HOW IT AFFECTS THE EMAIL):
Propensity does not change the one-solution rule. It changes how assertive, exploratory, or light the email should be, including the CTA.

Act Now (High Fit):
- Behaviour: direct, confident, practical, time-aware without pressure.
- Email depth: specific outcomes and immediate progress.
- CTA: short call, quick assessment, or next-step discussion.

Evaluate (Medium Fit):
- Behaviour: exploratory and evidence-based; validate relevance.
- Email depth: frame trade-offs, ask clarifying questions gently.
- CTA: discovery conversation, fit assessment, options review.

Nurture (Low Fit):
- Behaviour: light, contextual, awareness-building without a meeting push.
- Email depth: outcomes and best-practice framing, not hard recommendations.
- CTA: optional resource-sharing or "happy to share perspective" ask.

Educate (Very Low Fit):
- Behaviour: high-level informational tone; no selling.
- Email depth: concept awareness and common patterns; minimal assumptions.
- CTA: very soft (e.g., "open to a short chat if useful") or none.
